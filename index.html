About me

Hi, I'm Shirley! I'm a 4th-year student at the University of California, Santa Barbara (UCSB), double majoring in Statistics & Data Science and Psychology & Brain Science. I have a deep passion for combining data-driven analysis with human behavior insights, and I'm particularly interested in applying this knowledge to biostatistics and health data analysis .

Currently, I work in labs where I assist with data organization, experiment design, and analysis, focusing on psychological and brain science research. My technical toolkit includes Python, R, SAS, SQL, and experience with platforms like PsychoPy, SPSS, and Matlab. I’m passionate about using statistical methods to answer real-world questions, especially in areas related to public health and healthcare.

Beyond academics, I love cooking and tasting food from different places. I also enjoy rock climbing and traveling—experiences that help me explore new perspectives and challenge myself. I’m excited about all the places I have yet to visit and the experiences I have yet to discover!
